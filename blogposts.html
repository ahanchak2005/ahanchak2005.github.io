My favourite USAMO Problem

Problem Statement : Let $n$ be an integer greater than 1. Suppose $2n$ points are given in the plane, no three of which are collinear. Suppose $ n$ of the given $2n$ points are colored blue and the other $n$ colored red. A line in the plane is called a balancing line if it passes through one blue and one red point and, for each side of the line, the number of blue points on that side is equal to the number of red points on the same side. Prove that there exist at least two balancing lines.

My favourite child-hood riddle

PROBLEM: The famous gold-fish of the fishery of your city has been stolen. Now, you, being the best detective of your city has been hired to solve the case. You suspect the five richest aristocrats of your city who live in 5 consecutive houses each with a different nationality, having different wall colours, having different pets, drinking different beverages and smoking different cigars. But you don't know anything else. But you can not visit any house randomly if you are not sure. So, investigating you get this set of infos about those 5 people. With the help of these can you say who is the thief?
